How to Apply for a Kenyan Work Permit: Step-by-Step Guide (2026)

Work Permit 📅 March 4, 2026 ✎ By Iterin Editorial Team ⏱ 8 min read

If you're planning to work or invest in Kenya, a valid Kenyan work permit is essential. Whether you're relocating for employment, launching a business, or working in a specialized sector, the Government of Kenya requires foreign nationals to secure a work permit before engaging in any gainful activity.

In this 2026 guide, we walk you through the types of work permits available, eligibility criteria, required documents, application steps, processing timelines, and expert tips to maximize your approval chances.

Types of Work Permits in Kenya

The Kenya Citizenship and Immigration Act, 2011 (Section 40) provides for the following classes of work permits:

ClassCategory
Class AMining or prospecting
Class BAgricultural and Animal Husbandry
Class CPrescribed Profession
Class DEmployment by a registered Kenyan entity
Class FManufacturing
Class GSpecific trade, business, or consultancy
Class IApproved religious or charitable work
Class KPersons of independent means (retirees)
Class MRefugees (processed through UNHCR)
Class NDigital Nomads
Class REast African Community Members

Step-by-Step Application Process

Step 1: Determine Your Permit Class

The first step is identifying which class of work permit applies to your situation. Class D is the most common for foreign employees; Class G is for investors and business owners.

Step 2: Gather Required Documents

  • Completed application form (eCitizen portal)
  • Valid passport (at least 6 months validity)
  • Two recent passport-size photographs
  • Certified academic and professional qualifications
  • Curriculum Vitae / resume
  • Employer's offer letter or employment contract (Class D)
  • Certificate of Incorporation (Class G)
  • KRA PIN certificate of the sponsoring entity
  • Business plan and proof of investment (Class G)
  • Certificate of Good Conduct from country of origin (apostilled)
  • Medical certificate from an approved physician

Step 3: Submit via eCitizen

All Kenyan work permit applications are processed through the eCitizen portal (ecitizen.go.ke). Create an account, complete the online form, upload scanned documents, and pay the applicable government fee.

"Incomplete applications are the leading cause of delays and rejections. Having an experienced immigration consultant review your file before submission significantly increases approval rates." — Iterin Immigration Team

Step 4: Pay Government Fees

Work permit fees vary by class and are payable through eCitizen. Class D permits typically attract KES 100,000 for a two-year permit. Fees are subject to change — confirm current rates at the time of application.

Step 5: Await Processing

Processing times range from 30 to 90 days depending on the permit class and current application volume. Class G permits for high-value investments may be expedited through KenInvest.

Step 6: Collection and Entry

Once approved, you receive notification via eCitizen. Class D permits require the employee to collect the physical permit before or upon entry. Class G holders receive a sticker/endorsement or permit card.

Common Reasons for Rejection

  • Incomplete or inconsistent documentation
  • Failure to demonstrate that the role cannot be filled by a Kenyan national
  • Insufficient proof of qualifications
  • Outstanding immigration violations
  • Mismatched information between documents

Tips to Maximize Approval

  • Engage an experienced immigration consultant to review your application
  • Ensure all documents are properly certified and apostilled where required
  • Provide a comprehensive business plan for Class G applications
  • Apply at least 2 months before your intended start date
  • Respond promptly to any queries from the Directorate of Immigration Services
