Entry Work Permits

We handle all classes of Kenyan work permits under the Kenya Citizenship and Immigration Act, 2011.

Obtaining Kenyan Work Permits

Work Permits under Section 40 — Kenya Citizenship and Immigration Act, 2011

From business endeavors to employment opportunities or even long-term residency, obtaining the right permit is crucial. We provide expert advice and assistance in procuring the ideal permit for your unique circumstances, ensuring you can live, work, or conduct business in Kenya with complete peace of mind.

A Work Permit is issued under the provisions of Section 40 of the Kenya Citizenship and Immigration Act, 2011, to enable foreign nationals to enter Kenya and engage in trade, prospecting, farming, business, employment, missionary activities, or to retire in Kenya.

Available Work Permit Classes

Kenya offers the following classes of work permits, each tailored to a specific category of applicant:

  • Class A — Mining and Processing
  • Class B — Agricultural and Animal Husbandry
  • Class C — Prescribed Profession
  • Class D — Employment
  • Class F — Manufacturing
  • Class G — Investment on Specific Trade
  • Class I — Religious Activities
  • Class K — Ordinary Residents
  • Class M — Refugees
  • Class N — Digital Nomads
  • Class P — NGO Officers in East Africa
  • Class Q — Paid Employees of Religious Organisations
  • Class R — East African Community Members
  • Exemption — Gazetted by the Government

How We Help You

Our experienced team assesses your specific situation and advises on the most appropriate permit class. We then handle all documentation, liaise with the Directorate of Immigration Services, and manage the full application on your behalf — ensuring compliance and minimizing delays.

Whether you are an employer bringing in a foreign employee or an individual seeking to invest or work in Kenya, we guide you through every step of the process.
